Free, public, and friendly: Hamilton Heights' rise in Mutual Aid combats covid hardships

By: Moses Bustos

Friday, October 16th, 2020

2020 has been a year of extraordinary hardship. As the world recovers from the first wave of coronavirus, economic decline and record unemployment disrupt the distribution of vital resources like food and healthcare. Low-income communities living in densely populated neighborhoods like Harlem are especially vulnerable to the fallout of the pandemic.

In response, communities all over NY are banding together to support one another through mutual aid – a practice of solidarity that seeks to solve issues like hunger through community engagement and social equity. Most mutual aid groups are not organized in traditional hierarchical structures and instead unite neighbors through a common sense of need and organization. Some mutual aid groups go as far as to label themselves anarchists to stress the importance of recognizing members as equals despite age, gender, race, background, ethnicity. Since the pandemic, some of the most popular forms of mutual aid involve public refrigerators and street cleanings operated by neighbors and open to anybody in need. Recently, Hamilton Heights got its own public fridges and local street cleanings to help the community recover.

One refrigerator, dubbed “The Friendly Fridge” was recently installed at 5977 Broadway and West 242nd street in Hamilton Heights.

Facebook groups like @HamiltonHeights coordinate neighborhood street cleanings where neighbors and community members get together to "love our neighborhood! Xox".

Sugar Hill also has its own community fridge located at Broadway and West 152nd street.

One public refrigerator found its home at the Word Up community bookstore located on the corner of 165th street and Amsterdam Ave near Washington Heights. The bookstore, which is closed due to the pandemic, continues to hold community events outside on the sidewalk – where the fridge is located.
